Research

The best way to start research is to figure out your countries stance on the topic at hand. Here are some useful links to get started on the basics of your country. Use whatever statistics you think will help you prove your point on the subject. Use any other websites.

CIA World Factbook MUN Countries

Make sure you fully understand your countries stance on the topic as well as potentially friendly or unfriendly countries to yours. Read your committees background guide carefully and research your countries beliefs specifically on the committee topic. It will also be useful to understand your countries socio-economic status and research some statistics for your countries imports and exports. Feel free to expand your research options to journals and recent news as well. Finally, make sure you are constantly thinking about a resolution to you committees problem.

Position Papers

School Name:
Committee Name:
Country Name:
Topic:

Introductory paragraph: Brief summary of the topic and the main issues surrounding it. Include your country’s stance on the issue and a short preview of any related policy that your country has instituted regarding the issue. Keep this paragraph succinct (approximately 4-6 sentences) as you will expand of these issues later in your paper.

Background Information: This paragraph is a more in-depth history of the problem and attempted solutions both locally in your nation and internationally. In essence, establish the significance and history of the problem. This paragraph should include:
• Origin of the problem
• Scope of the problem. Why does it matter on an international scale?
• Any relevant statistical data pertaining to the issue both globally and in your specific nation
• Previously implemented solution by the United Nations and by your nation
• Reasons for success or failure of the previously attempted solutions

Possible solutions to the program: This paragraph is the key part of the position paper. List potential solutions to the issue and how the solutions would be implemented. Remember, you may not have a pre-written resolution, but this paragraph can be used to spark debate and support for your solutions. This paragraph should include:
• Specific proposals on how to address the issue
• The feasibility of the solutions and how they would be implemented
• Global impact of the solutions

Concluding paragraph: Concisely summarize the issue and your primary proposed solutions. This paragraph should be no more than 4-5 sentences.

[Identify committee, resolution number, and topic number in the header]
General Assembly 3.1 Topic 2

Sponsors [Delegates who physically write and contribute ideas]: United States, Germany, and Japan
Signatories [Delegates who would like to see the resolution debated, must be 15% of the room]: Italy, Mexico, Canada, the Netherlands, Chad
Topic: “Strengthening UN coordination of humanitarian assistance in complex emergencies”

The General Assembly,
[The first section of the resolution is the preamble, which points out the problem and background information on the topic. Each clause starts with a preambulatory phrase (see list below) and ends with a comma. A resolution typically has three to five preambulatory clauses.]
Noting with satisfaction the past efforts of various relevant UN bodies and nongovernmental organizations,
Stressing the fact that the United Nations faces significant financial obstacles and is in need of reform, particularly in the humanitarian realm,
[The second part of the resolution comprises operative clauses, which propose the actions that delegates believe need to be taken to address the topic. Just as with the preamble, each clause in this section starts with an operative phrase (see below) and ends with a semicolon, except the last clause, which will end with a period. The number of operative clauses depends on the sponsors; resolutions should have as many clauses as necessary to get the point across.]
1. Encourages all relevant agencies of the United Nations to collaborate more closely with countries at the grassroots level to enhance the carrying out of relief efforts;
2. Urges member states to comply with the goals of the UN Department of Humanitarian Affairs to streamline efforts of humanitarian aid;
3. Calls upon states to respond quickly and generously to consolidated appeals for humanitarian assistance.
