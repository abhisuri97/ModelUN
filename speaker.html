Keynote Speaker

James Francis Creagan

James Francis Creagan is a graduate of the University of Notre Dame (Class of 1962) and holds a doctorate from the University of Virginia. Ambassador Creagan began his career at the Agency for International Development under President Kennedy before being appointed to the Foreign Service by President Lyndon B. Johnson. Serving under nine U.S. presidents, Ambassador Creagan has more than thirty years of experience as a career diplomat.

Ambassador Creagan was nominated to be the United States Ambassador to Honduras by President Bill Clinton in 1996. Additionally, Ambassador Creagan was also asked to serve as a ChargeÃÅ d'Affaires in Bolivia in 2009 under President Obama. These are only a few of the positions held by Ambassador Creagan, as he has also served as Consul General, Political Counselor, and political/labor officer. In 2009, Ambassador Creagan acted as an international observer for presidential elections in Honduras.

After retiring from the foreign service, Ambassador Creagan became the president of John Cabot University in Rome, Italy. Today, in addition to lecturing extensively throughout the United States, Ambassador Creagan is currently a professor of International Affairs and Ambassador in Residence at the University of the Incarnate Word.
